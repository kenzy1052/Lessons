Introduction to Food and Nutrition

The Importance of Food

Food is one of the most essential things needed by all living organisms, especially humans, to live and function properly. It is any substance that we eat or drink which provides the body with the materials and energy it needs to grow, repair worn-out tissues, and stay healthy. Without food, the body cannot work properly; it becomes weak, sick, and may eventually die. Every single activity we perform — walking, talking, learning, or even sleeping — depends on the energy we obtain from the food we eat.

Did You Know?

Humans can live for days without food but cannot survive long without water. Water is one of the most important nutrients for our bodies!

Types of Nutrients in Food

Main Nutrients in Food

Food contains substances known as nutrients, and these nutrients perform specific functions in our bodies. The main nutrients in food include carbohydrates, proteins, fats and oils, vitamins, mineral salts, and water.

Detailed Information on Nutrients

Carbohydrates
Function: Main sources of energy for the body
Benefits: Give us strength to work, play, and do daily activities
Examples: Rice, yam, cassava, maize, bread, plantain, potatoes
Proteins
Function: Body-building foods
Benefits: Help the body grow and repair worn-out tissues
Examples: Meat, fish, beans, eggs, groundnuts, milk
Fats and Oils
Function: Provide concentrated energy
Benefits: Keep the body warm and protect delicate organs
Examples: Palm oil, margarine, butter, avocado pear, coconut, groundnut oil
Vitamins
Function: Protective foods that help resist diseases
Benefits: Needed in small quantities but very important for health
Examples: Oranges, mangoes, tomatoes, leafy greens
Mineral Salts
Function: Needed for strong bones, teeth, and blood formation
Benefits: Calcium for bones, iron for red blood cells
Examples: Fish, milk, eggs, salt, green vegetables, liver
Water
Function: Essential for many body processes
Benefits: Helps in digestion, circulation, temperature control, and waste removal
Importance: Humans can survive longer without food than without water

The Process of Digestion in Humans

What is Digestion?

Definition of Digestion

Digestion is the process of breaking down food into simpler, soluble substances that can be absorbed into the bloodstream. When we eat, the food cannot be used directly by the body; it has to be broken down into forms that can be transported to various parts of the body.

The Digestive System

The digestion of food in humans takes place in a long tube-like structure called the alimentary canal or digestive system. The main parts of this system include the mouth, oesophagus, stomach, small intestine, large intestine, rectum, and anus.

Step-by-Step Digestion Process

1
Mouth: The teeth chew and grind food into smaller pieces (mechanical digestion). Salivary glands produce saliva containing salivary amylase, which begins breaking down starch into sugar. The tongue mixes food with saliva and helps with swallowing.
2
Oesophagus: After swallowing, food passes through this muscular tube. The walls contract rhythmically to push food down to the stomach in a wave-like movement called peristalsis.
3
Stomach: Food is mixed with gastric juice containing enzymes that break down proteins. Hydrochloric acid in the stomach kills germs. Food stays in the stomach for several hours, becoming semi-liquid.
4
Small Intestine: Most digestion and absorption occur here. The liver produces bile to break down fats, and the pancreas produces enzymes to complete digestion of starch, proteins, and fats. The walls have tiny finger-like projections called villi that absorb digested food into the bloodstream.
5
Large Intestine: Water and some minerals are absorbed here. The remaining waste material becomes solid and is stored in the rectum.
6
Rectum and Anus: Solid waste (faeces) is stored in the rectum and later expelled through the anus during defaecation.

End Products of Digestion

Final Products of Digestion

The final products of digestion are simple substances that can dissolve in blood and be transported easily. Different nutrients are broken down into different end products:

Carbohydrates
Food Examples: Yam, rice, bread
End Product: Glucose (simple sugar)
Function: Used by cells to release energy
Proteins
Food Examples: Meat, beans, fish
End Product: Amino acids
Function: Used to build new tissues and repair damaged ones
Fats and Oils
Food Examples: Butter, palm oil, groundnuts
End Product: Fatty acids and glycerol
Function: Stored as body fat and used for energy when needed

Absorption and Utilization

How the Body Uses Digested Food

The simple products of digestion are absorbed into the blood through the villi of the small intestine. Once in the bloodstream, these nutrients are transported to all parts of the body where they are used to:

  • Produce energy for daily activities
  • Build new cells and tissues
  • Repair damaged tissues
  • Support growth and development
  • Maintain body functions
Important Note

The undigested parts of food are excreted as waste, keeping the body clean and balanced. This is why regular bowel movements are important for good health.

Practical Activities

Testing for Nutrients in Food

There are simple experiments that can be done to test for the presence of nutrients in food:

1
Test for Starch: Add a few drops of iodine solution to a food sample. If starch is present, the colour changes to blue-black.
2
Test for Glucose: Add Benedict's solution to a food sample and heat it. If glucose is present, the colour changes to orange or brick-red.
3
Test for Protein: Add biuret reagent to a food sample. A purple or violet colour shows that protein is present.
4
Test for Fats/Oils: Rub a bit of food on brown paper. If a greasy patch appears that does not dry, it shows that fat or oil is present.
Teaching Tip

These tests help us identify the nutrients that different foods contain and help us understand why it is important to eat a balanced diet. Try these tests with common foods like bread, milk, eggs, and various fruits and vegetables.

Key Points to Remember
Food and Nutrition:

Final Tip

To live a healthy and productive life, humans must eat balanced meals and maintain a healthy digestive system. Good eating habits, clean food, and plenty of water are essential for the proper functioning of the body.
