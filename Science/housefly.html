Introduction to Houseflies: All you need to know

1. Introduction

The housefly is a common insect that we see in homes, markets, and around food.

Key Facts

Scientific name: Musca domestica

Classification: They are considered pests because they can spread diseases.

2. Life Cycle of the Housefly

The life cycle is the series of stages a housefly goes through from birth to adulthood. The life cycle has four main stages:

Egg
Larva
Pupa
Adult
Tip for students

The life cycle is continuous: Adult flies lay eggs, which hatch into larvae, then become pupae, and finally emerge as adult flies that lay eggs again.

2.1 Egg Stage

  • Houseflies lay eggs in decaying food, manure, or garbage.
  • Each female can lay 100–150 eggs at a time.
  • Eggs are small, white, and oval-shaped.
  • Eggs hatch within 1 day into larvae.
Tip for students

Think of eggs as the "baby stage" of the fly.

2.2 Larva Stage (Maggot)

  • After hatching, the fly becomes a larva, also called a maggot.
  • Larvae are soft, white, and legless.
  • They feed on decaying food and waste, growing bigger quickly.
  • This stage lasts about 3–5 days.
Tip for students

The larva is like the "childhood" of the fly, growing fast and eating a lot.

2.3 Pupa Stage

  • After the larva stage, it forms a pupa.
  • Pupae are brownish, hard, and oval-shaped.
  • Inside the pupa, the fly changes into an adult.
  • This stage lasts about 3–6 days.
Tip for students

The pupa is like a cocoon, a resting and changing stage before adulthood.

2.4 Adult Stage

  • The adult fly emerges from the pupa.
  • It has wings, eyes, and legs, ready to fly and reproduce.
  • Adult flies live about 15–30 days.
  • They feed on sugar, liquids, food scraps, and waste.
Important Note

The complete life cycle from egg to adult can take as little as 7-10 days under ideal conditions, which is why housefly populations can grow rapidly.

3. Activities of Houseflies and Their Impact

Houseflies can cause problems for humans because of their feeding habits.

3.1 Feeding Habits

Houseflies feed on:

Common Food Sources
Dead animals
Rotten food
Manure (animal poop)
Solid and liquid waste

They use their sticky feet and mouthparts to pick up food.

3.2 How They Affect Humans

Impact Description
Transfer of diseases They carry bacteria and viruses on their body and legs. Example: Dysentery and cholera.
Food poisoning Flies land on food and leave germs behind.
Nuisance They are annoying and disturb people in homes and restaurants.
Health Risk

Houseflies can carry over 100 different pathogens that can cause diseases in humans and animals.

4. How to Reduce the Effects of Houseflies

We can control houseflies to keep ourselves safe:

Prevention Methods
Keep food covered
Dispose of waste properly
Clean kitchen and surroundings regularly
Use fly traps, insect sprays, or fly screens
Educate people in your community about hygiene and waste management
Tip for students

Think of houseflies as a warning to keep our environment clean.

5. Summary

  • The life cycle of a housefly has 4 stages: egg → larva → pupa → adult.
  • Houseflies are menaces to humans because they spread diseases, contaminate food, and cause nuisance.
  • We can reduce their effects by keeping our surroundings clean and hygienic.

6. Fun Activity

Draw flashcards for each stage of the housefly.

Activity Steps
Draw or print pictures of each stage: egg, larva, pupa, adult
Write the name of each stage on the back of the card
Arrange them in the correct order: egg → larva → pupa → adult
Use arrows to show the cycle is continuous

You can also create a 3D model of the housefly life cycle using clay or other craft materials.
