1. What is a Ratio?

A ratio is a way of comparing two or more quantities. It tells us "how much of one thing there is compared to another."

Key Point

üëâ Ratios can be written in three ways:

  • Using a colon : ‚Üí e.g., 3:2
  • As a fraction ‚Üí e.g., 3/2
  • In words ‚Üí e.g., "3 to 2"
Example 1:

There are 60 boys and 120 girls in a school.

The ratio of boys to girls = 60:120

We simplify by dividing both numbers by 60 ‚Üí 1:2

So, the ratio of boys to girls is 1:2.

This means: For every 1 boy, there are 2 girls.

Example 2:

At Kumasi Zoo, each bird has 2 wings and 1 beak.

The ratio of wings to beaks = 2:1

This means: For every 2 wings, there is 1 beak.

Example 3:

Musa is 25 years old, and his father Alhasan is 50 years old.

The ratio of Musa's age to Alhasan's age is 25:50 = 1:2.

This means:

  • Alhasan is twice as old as Musa.
  • Musa is half the age of his father.
Key Point

‚úÖ Ratios can describe relationships between quantities in very simple terms.

2. Unit Rate

Sometimes we want to know the amount of one quantity for just one unit of another quantity. This is called a unit rate.

Example 1:

The ratio is 3:4.

As a unit rate: 3 √∑ 4 = 0.75 cups of flour per cup of sugar.

So, for each 1 cup of sugar, you need 0.75 cups of flour.

Example 2:

3. Equivalent Ratios

Ratios are like fractions. We can find equivalent ratios by multiplying or dividing both sides by the same number.

Example 1:

Kafui (36 years), Adoley (48 years), Jantuah (24 years) share money in the ratio of their ages.

  • Kafui ‚Üí 36 √∑ 12 = 3
  • Adoley ‚Üí 48 √∑ 12 = 4
  • Jantuah ‚Üí 24 √∑ 12 = 2

    • So, ratio = 3:4:2

    • üëâ Jantuah = 2 √ó 12,000 = 24,000

4. Proportional Reasoning

When two ratios are equal, we call it a proportion.

Example 1 (Find missing value):

B 10 x 30

Solution:

  • 3 ‚Üí 10

Thus, x = 20.

5. Percentages as Ratios

A percent means "out of 100". So, 30% = 30/100.

Summary
  • A ratio compares two or more quantities.
  • A unit rate tells us how much of one quantity per 1 unit of another.
  • Ratios can be written as equivalent ratios.
  • Proportions are when two ratios are equal.
  • A percent is a ratio out of 100.
Tip for Students
