Punctuation and Capitalisation ‚Äî Teaching Notes for JHS 1

1. Overview: What Are We Learning?

Key Definitions

Punctuation: Marks used in writing to help readers understand the sentence clearly and correctly.

Capitalisation: Using capital letters where needed, such as at the beginning of sentences, for names, and for pronouns.

2. Punctuation: Key Marks & Rules

A. Full Stop ( . )

Use to end a statement.

Example: "She is reading a book."
Practice: Add full stops to: "the dog is running"
B. Question Mark ( ? )

Use at the end of questions.

Example: "Where are you going?"
Practice: Turn into questions: "you like school"
C. Exclamation Mark ( ! )

Shows strong feeling or excitement.

Example: "Watch out!"
Practice: Add exclamation marks: "What a beautiful bird"
D. Comma ( , )

Separates items in a list or parts of a sentence to prevent confusion.

Example: "I bought pens, books, and erasers."
Practice: Insert commas: "I have apples bananas mangoes"

3. Capitalisation: Rules & Examples

A. First Word of a Sentence

Always capitalise.

Example: "Tomorrow we will visit."
B. The Pronoun "I"

Always written as a capital letter, no matter where it is in a sentence.

Example: "I am happy."
C. The Interjection "O"

When used to call or address someone, write O with a capital.

Example: "O Teacher, may we ask a question?"
D. Names of People, Animals, Places, and Heavenly Bodies

Always capitalised.

People: "Ama, Kofi"
Animals (as names): "Zoomer the dog"
Planets: "Earth, Mars"
Places: "Accra, Ghana"

4. Combined Examples: Punctuation + Capitalisation

Sentence Description
"O children, look at the stars." "O" is capitalised as an interjection; comma separates direct address; full stop ends the sentence.
"I like Accra, Kumasi, and Tamale." "I" is capitalised; commas separate items in a list; full stop ends the sentence.
"Where is the moon?" Question mark after a question; "Where" is capitalised because it's the first word.
"What a bright star!" Exclamation mark shows excitement; first word is capitalised.

  • Capitalised O (interjection).

6. Classroom Tips

Teaching Tips
  • Visual Aids: Draw symbols for punctuation marks and display examples on the board.
  • Interactive Practice: Get students to punctuate and capitalise in pairs.
  • Error Correction: Present common mistakes and ask students to find and correct them.
  • Reinforcement: End each lesson by asking a few students to orally explain one rule.
  • Short Homework: Ask students to write 3‚Äì5 simple sentences using various punctuation marks and capital letters.

7. Recap of Key Rules

Punctuation
  • Full stop (.) for statements
  • Question mark (?) for questions
  • Exclamation mark (!) for strong feelings
  • Comma (,) for lists or clarity
Capitalisation
  • First word of each sentence
  • Pronoun I
  • Interjection O
  • Names (people, animals, places, planets)

Vocabulary ‚Äî JHS 1 Teaching Notes

Reference:

B7.3.3.1.1: Apply vocabulary appropriately in specific contexts.
B7.3.4.1.1: Explore the use of proverbs to enrich communication.

1. Lesson Objectives

By the end of the lesson, pupils should be able to:

  • Use appropriate vocabulary in specific contexts and situations (e.g., asking for advice, ordering food).
  • Apply useful expressions when agreeing and disagreeing.
  • Use vocabulary correctly in social settings (e.g., at a friend's birthday, in conversations with adults and peers).
  • Identify and use proverbs to enrich their speech and writing.
  • Demonstrate communication, critical thinking, and presentation skills in role plays and group work.

2. Core Competencies Developed

Competency Description
Communication and Collaboration Using correct expressions in dialogue and group practice.
Personal Development and Leadership Expressing oneself confidently in real-life situations.
Creativity and Innovation Choosing different words and proverbs to make speech interesting.
Critical Thinking and Problem-Solving Selecting the best vocabulary for the right situation.
Cultural Identity and Global Citizenship Applying Ghanaian and English proverbs.
Digital Literacy Using digital tools (dictionary apps, online thesaurus) to learn new words.
Presentation Performing role plays and presenting corrected dialogues.

3. Lesson Content

A. Vocabulary in Contexts
1. When Asking for and Giving Advice
Asking for advice

  • What do you think I should do?
  • What would you recommend?

2. When Agreeing and Disagreeing
Agreeing

  • I'm with you on that point.
  • I'll go along with that.
Disagreeing

3. When Ordering Food in a Restaurant
  • Can I see the menu, please?
  • I would like some fried rice with chicken.
  • Could I have a bottle of water, please?
  • How much is the meal?
  • Thank you for the service.
4. When Speaking at a Friend's Birthday Party
  • I'm happy to be here today.
  • Let's all celebrate our dear friend.
  • I wish you good health, success, and happiness.
  • May this year bring you joy and peace.
  • Congratulations on your birthday!
5. When in Conversation with Peers and Adults
With peers (informal):
  • Hey, how are you doing?
  • That was fun!
  • Let's play after school.
With adults (formal):
  • Good morning, Madam. How are you?
  • Please, may I ask a question?
  • Thank you for your guidance.

4. Classroom Activities

Role Play (Communication & Collaboration)

Students act out a situation of asking for and giving advice.

Example: A friend lost his school bag ‚Äî what advice can others give?
Debate (Agreeing & Disagreeing)

Motion: "Mobile phones should be allowed in school."

Students practise expressions of agreement and disagreement.
Restaurant Simulation (Creativity & Innovation)

Classroom turns into a "restaurant."

Students take turns being waiter/waitress and customer, ordering food using correct expressions.
Speech at a Birthday Party (Presentation)

Pupils prepare a short speech for a friend's birthday and deliver it in front of the class.

Proverb Matching Game (Cultural Identity)

Teacher writes English proverbs on one side and Ghanaian equivalents on the other.

Students match them correctly and use them in sentences.

5. Assessment (Evaluation)

Students will:

  • Use appropriate vocabulary in role plays (advice, agreement/disagreement, ordering food).
  • Write a short birthday message with correct expressions.
  • Match English proverbs with Ghanaian ones and use them in sentences.
  • Demonstrate correct choice of formal/informal vocabulary when speaking to peers or adults.

6. Summary (Key Takeaways)

Key Takeaways
  • Vocabulary must fit the context: advice, discussion, ordering food, birthday, formal/informal talk.
  • Proverbs make speech richer and more meaningful.
  • Choosing the right words shows respect, intelligence, and confidence.
  • Communication is not just about speaking, but about speaking appropriately and effectively.
